About

I am a graduate student at NYU, working under Prof. Stefano Martiniani. I am working on finding long-term memory formation in mice in collaboration with Prof. Andr√© Fenton. The question we are asking is, based on the Transcriptomic data and long-term behavioral data, is it possible to identify the genes responsible for long-term memory formation? I am using Information Theory and Mutual Information to find the correlation between behavior and gene expressions.

My Research interest lies in understanding the Human Brain, how the brain perceives the world, How it encodes information from external stimuli, makes sense of the sensory world, and predicts the correct behavior for a given situation. I am interested in building a theoretical model for the Learning process in humans.

I got my master's in Chemistry from the Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur, India. I worked with Prof. Nisanth Nair on developing algorithms for Enhanced Sampling Method TASS. I worked as a research assistant at TIFR Hyderabad under Prof. Jagannath Mondal on finding collective variables for the drug-binding problem using Deep Learning methods like Autoencoder, Variational-Autoencoder, and Generative Adversarial Network.
